Commonly found throughout most of Scoria, hence the name. These birds primarily eat small seeds and fruits, but will also eat insects and green vegetation if no other options are provided. Birds found closer to the population centers will also consume litter and food waste. Common trillers mate multiple times in one season, but only produce one clutch of four to six eggs. The young are reared by the mother for three months, then left to live on their own. The birds are gregarious and form flocks of, on average, 50 individuals. Their song consists of a simple repeated melody that varies between individuals.
